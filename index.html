Introduction

                When an object surpasses the speed of sound, sound waves emitted by the aircraft pile up and form one single shockwave. This results in the sonic boom.

                This sonic boom is the reason why non-military aircraft cannot exceed the speed of sound over land, as the shockwave generated can cause structural damage and disrupt wildlife.

                However, new advancements in technology and a new approach to aircraft design are leading the way to supersonic aircraft producing a lower decibel or negligible sonic boom.
